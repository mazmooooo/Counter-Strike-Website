Counter Strike Source



About



Counter-Strike: Source was released on October 7, 2004. It was developed by Valve Corporation, using their new Source engine (the same engine later used for games like Half-Life 2). Counter-Strike: Source is a remake of the classic Counter-Strike (mainly 1.6), but with upgraded graphics, physics, and mechanics thanks to the Source engine. While it stayed true to the original's gameplay — terrorists vs. counter-terrorists — it introduced a much more modern look and feel. Some players loved the smoother visuals and physics, while others preferred the "raw" competitive style of CS 1.6. CS: Source also helped bridge the gap to newer games like Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO).
